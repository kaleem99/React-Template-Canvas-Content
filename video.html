2. Business risks and opportunities

Businesses are becoming acutely aware of the inherent risks of doing business on a global scale. Their dependence on diverse natural resources for raw materials, an international customer base, and a heavy reliance on information and communication technologies all need to be carefully managed if plans for growth are not to be undermined. Additionally, companies need to grasp the new commercial opportunities that addressing sustainability challenges present and need to be agile enough to adapt their business models accordingly to accommodate the global shifts ahead.

In Video 1 Part 1, Louise Nicholls, an online tutor and guest lecturer at CISL, speaks about the societal impacts of a company in relation to business risk and opportunities. She highlights several examples of social sustainability issues that businesses deal with and the role that businesses have to play in addressing social sustainability.
